Terms & Conditions

  1. Salary Guarantee
    1. Guaranteed Monthly Compensation
      • Regardless of the actual number of days worked within any given month, all employed nurses are entitled to receive their full monthly salary. This condition is intended to ensure financial stability and job security for our staff.
    2. Conditions for Salary Guarantee
      • This salary guarantee is conditional upon the nurse being assigned to at least one active duty during the month. If the nurse is not assigned or available for work, the guarantee may not apply, subject to the provisions outlined in Section 3 on Employment Status and Termination.
  2. Service Termination by Clients
    1. Notification Requirement for Service Termination
      • Clients wishing to terminate nursing services must provide a minimum of one week's notice prior to the intended termination date. This advance notice is crucial to allow adequate time for administrative adjustments and to ensure the continuity of care for all clients.
    2. Consequences of Failing to Provide Notice
      • Failure to comply with the notification requirement as stated in Section 2.1 may result in the client being liable for the payment of services that would have been rendered during the notice period. This is to ensure that nurses are compensated for the scheduled time that could have been allocated to other assignments.
  3. Employment Status and Termination
    1. Terms of Employment
      • Nurses are employed on a monthly basis, with their continuation of employment contingent upon the fulfillment of their duties and compliance with all applicable policies and standards as set forth by the employer.
    2. Employer Termination Rights
      • The employer reserves the right to terminate employment under circumstances including, but not limited to, non-compliance with healthcare regulations, breach of contract, or failure to meet professional standards. Standard termination policies including notice periods and severance (if applicable) will be adhered to, in compliance with local labor laws.
